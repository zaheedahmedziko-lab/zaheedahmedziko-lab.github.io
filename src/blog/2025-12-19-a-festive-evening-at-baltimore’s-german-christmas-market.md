As the semester came to a close, I had the opportunity to join Towson University’s International Student and Scholar Office (ISSO) trip to the German Christmas Market at Baltimore’s Inner Harbor. The event offered a refreshing break after a busy academic term and created a warm space for international students to connect, celebrate, and enjoy the holiday atmosphere together.

The trip took place on December 18, with participants meeting at the Residence Tower before departing for Baltimore in the evening. After weeks of classes, assignments, and final exam preparation, the visit felt like a meaningful way to pause and enjoy the end of the semester with friends and fellow students.

At the market, the lights, music, local vendors, seasonal treats, and festive decorations created a lively winter setting. Walking through the market with the group made the experience even more memorable. It was not only a chance to explore one of Baltimore’s popular seasonal attractions but also an opportunity to build community beyond the classroom.

One of the best parts of the evening was the sense of togetherness. As an international student, I find that events like this make campus life feel more welcoming. They provide a chance to meet people, share experiences, and create memories outside the academic environment. The group photos from the trip capture that feeling well: a simple but meaningful evening of friendship, celebration, and community.

I am grateful to Towson University, the International Student and Scholar Office, and Housing & Residence Life for organizing this trip. It was a memorable way to end the semester and a reminder of how important community-building experiences are in the student journey.
