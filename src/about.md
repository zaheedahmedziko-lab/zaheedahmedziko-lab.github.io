## Academic Background

I am a Ph.D. student in Information Technology at Towson University, Maryland, USA, and a Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Computer and Information Sciences.

My academic journey has been shaped by a strong interest in cybersecurity and its role in modern digital infrastructure. My work connects cybersecurity with emerging computing systems, including AI-enabled systems, IoT, cloud computing, software-defined networking, and secure intelligent systems.

I completed my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering with a major in Cybersecurity from United International University, Bangladesh. During my master’s studies, I focused on cybersecurity, network security, cloud computing, cloud security, digital forensics, cybercrime, ethical hacking, internet architecture, and future internet technologies.

Before moving further into computer science and cybersecurity research, I completed my B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology, where my major area was power systems.

## Research Identity

My research interests include cybersecurity, AI security, AI agent security, software-defined networking, IoT and cloud security, secure embedded systems, healthcare technologies, and green computing.

My publication and research experience includes cloud-IoT healthcare systems, SDN control-plane security, NB-IoT and edge intelligence for healthcare, nanosatellite communication, and AI-enabled systems.

## Academic Direction

My long-term academic goal is to contribute to secure, trustworthy, and resilient computing systems. I am especially interested in research that connects theory, practical experimentation, and real-world cybersecurity challenges.
