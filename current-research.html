Current Work

Current Research

My current research direction focuses on cybersecurity, AI security, AI agent security, trustworthy tool-using systems, network security, and secure intelligent computing.

Security of AI Agents and Tool-Using Systems

I am interested in the security risks of AI agents that interact with external tools, files, APIs, and user environments. A key direction is detecting and preventing indirect prompt injection attacks, where malicious instructions are hidden inside external content and later influence an AI agent’s behavior.

Focus Areas

  • Indirect prompt injection in tool-using AI agents
  • Runtime enforcement and guardrail design
  • Separation of detection and action-level prevention
  • Secure agent architecture and trustworthy AI workflows
  • Evaluation of attack success rate, detection accuracy, and blocking behavior

Cybersecurity and Network Security

My broader cybersecurity interests include network security, software-defined networking, secure control-plane design, attack analysis, privacy-aware communication, and practical defense mechanisms for emerging cyber threats.

Focus Areas

  • Software-defined networking security
  • Control-plane threats and countermeasures
  • Network attack analysis and defense mechanisms
  • Secure and privacy-aware communication systems

IoT, Cloud, and Healthcare Security

My previous and continuing research connects IoT, cloud computing, edge intelligence, and healthcare technologies. This includes scalable healthcare architectures, green computing, and secure real-time monitoring systems.

Focus Areas

  • Cloud-IoT healthcare systems
  • NB-IoT and edge intelligence
  • Secure healthcare monitoring
  • Energy-aware and scalable computing systems

Future Research Direction

My long-term goal is to contribute to secure, trustworthy, and resilient computing systems by combining cybersecurity research with AI-enabled technologies, networked systems, and practical defense-oriented experimentation.
