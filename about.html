Profile

About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Information Technology at Towson University, Maryland, USA, with academic and research interests in cybersecurity, AI security, IoT, cloud computing, software-defined networking, and secure intelligent systems.

Academic Background

My academic journey has been shaped by a strong interest in cybersecurity and its role in modern digital infrastructure. I am currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Information Technology at Towson University and serving as a Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Computer and Information Sciences.

I completed my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering with a major in Cybersecurity from United International University, Bangladesh. During my master’s studies, I focused on cybersecurity, network security, cloud computing, cloud security, digital forensics, cybercrime, ethical hacking, internet architecture, and future internet technologies.

Before moving further into computer science and cybersecurity research, I completed my B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology, where my major area was power systems. This background gave me exposure to communication systems, digital electronics, microprocessors, signal processing, and engineering problem-solving.

Research Identity

My research interests connect cybersecurity with emerging computing systems. I am particularly interested in AI security, AI agent security, software-defined networking, IoT and cloud security, secure embedded systems, healthcare technologies, and green computing.

My publication and research experience includes cloud-IoT healthcare systems, SDN control plane security, NB-IoT and edge intelligence for healthcare, nanosatellite communication, and AI-enabled systems.
